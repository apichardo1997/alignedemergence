Hey, I'm Anabel

I spent way too much time trying to figure out the perfect way to explain what this blog means to me— why I started it, why I care, and why you might want to stick around. But I kept getting stuck trying to perfectly express my passion for questions around the future of tech… instead of just starting the journey.

So, here it is: I’m Anabel. I have a background in finance, a deep fascination with economics and technology, and a brain that won’t stop chasing questions.

Why I’m Here

I believe if we embrace technology thoughtfully, we can grow from it—and with it. But if we reject it or ignore it, we risk something deeper: not just being left behind, but slowly losing our humanity to it.

I’m just a scientist, trying to hypothesize where this AI race is headed. And honestly? I have way more questions than answers.

Some of my thoughts come from curiosity—and from the limited information we currently have about AI safety, ethics, and how machines actually learn.

Is the fear around AI just humanity’s 100th doomsday theory?
Or is it something more real—like discovering fire and not knowing whether it will warm us or burn us to the ground?

That’s what I want to explore. Not with certainty, but with questions. With you.

Why AI Feels So Human

Beyond buzzwords like “Technological Singularity,” there are real-time conversations we need to be having about AI. Conversations that don’t just belong to engineers or CEOs—but to all of us.

What pulled me in the most is that AI is inherently interdisciplinary. It’s the first field I’ve found where all my curiosities converge. They emerge.

This blog is where I get to explore all the frameworks, theories, and side-quests I’ve been following for years— and finally bring them into conversation with something bigger. With you.

A Few Ground Rules

I’m not here to be an expert. I’m not here to die on any theoretical hills.

If you disagree with something I say—connect with me! Enlighten me! That’s the whole point. Everything I write comes from a place of respect—whether I touch on religion, gender, mental health, or anything else. These are my opinions, shaped by my lens. I hope yours are rooted in critical thinking too. Let’s trade lenses sometimes.

PS: A Note on How I Write

I bow to keep my articles as close to human as possible. Yes, I use generative AI to help me clean up structure and grammar—but I want every conversation here to sound like me, not a bot. If you ever feel like something sounds robotic, call me out. I’m learning too.
